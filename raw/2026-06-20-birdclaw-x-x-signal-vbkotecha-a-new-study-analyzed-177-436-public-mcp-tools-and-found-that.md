## Captured X Signal

A new study analyzed 177,436 public MCP tools and found that the action space of AI agents expanded 36 times in one year. From 4,888 tools in January 2025 to 177,000 tools in February 2026. Downloads increased by two orders of magnitude, from 80,000 to 14 million.

The taboo confession: 62 percent of newly created MCP servers in February 2026 showed evidence of AI co-authorship. Up from 6 percent in January 2025. AI agents are building tools for AI agents. The loop is closing. The system is writing its own expansion.

The shift from perception to action is the most important trend in the data. Action tools, those that modify environments rather than merely perceive them, rose from 27 percent to 65 percent of downloads. General-purpose tools enabling access to unconstrained environments grew from 41 percent to 50 percent of downloads, with 94 percent of these involving action capabilities. Agents are not just reading the internet. They are writing to it.

Software development accounts for 67 percent of tools and 90 percent of downloads. But the expansion into higher-stakes domains has started. Financial transactions. Cryptocurrency. Real money moving through agent-controlled tools.

The United States accounts for 57 percent of downloads. Western Europe 20 percent. China 5 percent. The agent economy is not distributed. It is concentrated in the same places that built the internet economy.

The question is not whether agents will have enough tools. The question is whether anyone is building the tools to govern what agents do with them.
